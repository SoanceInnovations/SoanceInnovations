Summary / TLDR

Loomora is a curated marketplace connecting independent craft makers with design-conscious buyers. The team needed a mobile marketplace that made product discovery, maker stories, multi-vendor inventory, and international checkout feel effortless.

Soance shaped the strategy, experience, and technical foundation around measurable user and operational needs, then translated the concept into a launch-ready digital product.

Overview

Loomora is a fictional artisan commerce company created for this representative portfolio story. Its operating context, user needs, and delivery constraints are modeled on realistic digital transformation engagements.

The core brief was to deliver a mobile marketplace that made product discovery, maker stories, multi-vendor inventory, and international checkout feel effortless while giving internal teams a system they could confidently operate, measure, and evolve after launch.

Services provided

  • Product strategy and user research
  • Mobile UX and interface design
  • Cross-platform application development
  • API integration, QA, and launch support

Challenges

The project needed to balance a polished customer experience with the less-visible realities of content, operations, permissions, integrations, and long-term maintainability.

  • Maker storefronts and origin stories: The experience needed to support this capability without adding unnecessary complexity for users or operational teams.
  • Multi-currency checkout and localized discovery: The experience needed to support this capability without adding unnecessary complexity for users or operational teams.
  • Vendor inventory and order management: The experience needed to support this capability without adding unnecessary complexity for users or operational teams.

Solution

We designed the experience around short, confidence-building tasks, then built a cross-platform application backed by secure APIs, analytics, and a maintainable design system.

  • Designed and validated maker storefronts and origin stories as part of one coherent, role-aware product journey.
  • Designed and validated multi-currency checkout and localized discovery as part of one coherent, role-aware product journey.
  • Designed and validated vendor inventory and order management as part of one coherent, role-aware product journey.

A shared design system, instrumentation plan, and staged release approach kept the product coherent while allowing the team to learn from realistic usage patterns.

Illustrative outcomes

Because Loomora is fictional, these outcomes describe the intended value of the solution rather than claims about a real client engagement.

  • A clearer mobile journey with fewer decision points
  • A scalable product foundation for new services and markets
  • More consistent support, analytics, and release operations
